This one-day symposium will bring together leading ophthalmologists, AI researchers, and industry experts to discuss the latest advancements in AI for eyecare.

Speakers

Dr. Murali Ariga

Head of Ophthalmology, Sundaram Medical Foundation

Talk Title: AI in Glaucoma: Scope and Possibilities for Future Collaborative Research

Bio: Dr. Murali Ariga is a distinguished ophthalmologist in Chennai, specializing in glaucoma, lasers, Minimally Invasive Glaucoma Surgery (MIGS), and phacoemulsification with premium Intraocular Lenses (IOLs). He serves as Director of Swamy Eye Clinic and Chennai Glaucoma Foundation and Senior Glaucoma Consultant at Dr. Agarwal's Eye Hospital. He also heads Ophthalmology at Sundaram Medical Foundation. Dr. Ariga holds a Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS), a Master of Surgery in Ophthalmology (MS), Diplomate of National Board (DNB), and is a Fellow of the All India Collegium of Ophthalmology (FAICO) in Glaucoma. He has international subspecialty training and community ophthalmology experience. With 44 publications, 10 book chapters, and the edited book "Practical Perimetry," he is a recognized authority. He contributes actively to research, including the CURES study, and presents globally. Dr. Ariga has received the Indian Journal of Ophthalmology (IJO) Silver Award and Tamil Nadu Ophthalmic Association (TNOA) Dr J. Agarwal's Excellence in Teaching Award. He champions glaucoma awareness through public programs, highlighting his commitment to clinical practice and public health.

Dr. Prasanna Venkatesh Ramesh

Research Director, Mahathma Eye Hospital

Talk Title: Artificial Intelligence In Ophthalmology - The Novel, Self-Built, Cost Effective & Multimodal AI Tool Box

Bio: Dr. Prasanna Venkatesh Ramesh is a leading ophthalmologist and glaucoma specialist, heading the Glaucoma Department and research initiatives at Mahathma Eye Hospital, Trichy, India. He holds a Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery, Master of Surgery, and Diplomate of National Board, along with fellowships in glaucoma and cataract surgery. As a Member of the National Academy of Medical Sciences and a Fellow of both the All India Collegium of Ophthalmology and the International Council of Ophthalmology, Dr. Ramesh has authored 52 articles and 12 book chapters, while serving on the editorial boards of the Indian Journal of Ophthalmology and the Tamil Nadu Ophthalmic Association Journal of Ophthalmic Sciences and Research. He actively participates in the Young Ophthalmologists Society of India and the Tamil Nadu Ophthalmic Association. A recognized instructor and recipient of numerous awards, including the "Dr. S.K. Sundaramoorthy Memorial Grand Prize" and the "Prof C.P. Gupta Award," Dr. Ramesh's research explores advancements in glaucoma treatment through surgical techniques, minimally invasive procedures, and technologies like artificial intelligence.

Dr. Yogish Kamath

Professor and Head of Department of Ophthalmology, Kasturba Medical College

Talk Title: Ophthalmology and Artificial Intelligence

Bio: Dr. Yogish Subraya Kamath is a Professor of Ophthalmology and currently heads the department at Kasturba Medical College-Manipal, India. He has an MBBS and MS (Ophthalmology) degree from Manipal University and completed his long-term Fellowship in Uveitis under Professor Rathinam, at the Aravind eye hospital, Madurai. His research interests include Ophthalmology and Systemic disease, Uveitis, and Ophthalmic diagnostics in Community. He has several research papers and grants on these topics. He is actively involved in teaching Ophthalmology to Undergraduates, Postgraduates and PhD students at his University, and is an anterior segment surgeon in the University hospital since 2006.

Head of AI & R&D, Carl Zeiss India

Talk Title: Impact of AI on Diagnosis Advancements: Vision-Related Systemic and Degenerative Diseases

Bio: Dr. Raghu Prasad leads AI & RD at Carl Zeiss India, with 12+ years of experience in digital healthcare. He's an expert in computer vision, AI, and machine learning, focusing on ophthalmology, oncology, and surgical navigation. Dr. Raghu has a strong record of innovation, holding 10 patents and authoring 25 publications. He previously held key roles at GE Healthcare and Philips Healthcare, where he spearheaded AI research initiatives and shaped product strategies. Dr. Raghu earned his Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from IIT Madras and completed post-doctoral work at the University of Calgary, specializing in AI-powered surgical solutions.

Bio: Dr. Sanjay is an experienced Operations Analyst and Data Engineer with a PhD in Electronics Engineering. Prof. Gaurav Sharma

University of Rochester, USA

Talk Title: New Tricks for an Old Dog: Combining Traditional Computer Vision with Deep Learning for Ophthalmological Image Analysis

Bio: Dr. Gaurav Sharma is a Professor at the University of Rochester, holding appointments in Electrical and Computer Engineering, Computer Science, and Biostatistics and Computational Biology. He is also a Distinguished Researcher in Data Science. Dr. Sharma previously directed the Center for Emerging and Innovative Sciences and worked as a Principal Scientist at Xerox. He holds a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from North Carolina State University and Masters degrees from NCSU and the Indian Institute of Science. A Fellow of IEEE, SPIE, and IS&T, Dr. Sharma has served as Editor-in-Chief for leading journals and has held numerous leadership positions within IEEE. His research interests include image processing, computer vision, and data analytics.

Prof. Thrasos Pappas

Northwestern University, USA

Talk Title: Generative Adversarial Networks for Visual Texture Modeling

Bio: Dr. Thrasyvoulos N. Pappas is a Professor in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at Northwestern University. His research focuses on perceptual models for multimedia processing, including visual, tactile, and acoustic texture analysis. He has made significant contributions to image and video compression, texture analysis for material identification, and content-based retrieval. Dr. Pappas is a Fellow of IEEE and SPIE, and has served as Editor-in-Chief for the IS&T Journal of Perceptual Imaging and the IEEE Transactions on Image Processing. He is actively involved in the SPIE/IS&T Human Vision and Electronic Imaging Conference and has given plenary talks at prestigious conferences like ICIP.

Dr. Shonraj Ballae Ganesh Rao

Talk Title: Designing AI for Scalable Population Health Screening: From Innovation to Regulatory Approval and Impact

Bio: Dr. Shonraj Ballae Ganeshrao is a highly experienced researcher and optometrist specializing in clinical research and AI-driven healthcare solutions. He currently leads clinical research at Remidio Innovative Solutions, where he validates new products and AI technologies. Dr. Ganeshrao holds a Ph.D. in Vision Science from the University of Melbourne, focusing on Bayesian algorithms for peripheral vision assessment. He previously earned optometry degrees from the Elite School of Optometry, BITS Pilani. With over 11 years in the field, Dr. Ganeshrao has secured grants from DST and SERB, and authored 30+ peer-reviewed articles. His expertise lies in bridging vision science with cutting-edge AI for enhanced healthcare outcomes.
